Pin the Bin is a interactive activity that encourages Young People to consider how many bins there are in an area. You can make use of the provided maps (from the Broxburn, West Lothian area) or you could do your own survey and use these results for the activity (see below for some instructions).

How to Play

  1. First introduce the game, a suggestion of introduction is below
  1. Show the first map without any icons, give the Young People 5 minutes or so to pin Bin icons on the map. The Young People should work together to decide where to put on the Bin icons on the map.
  1. Once the time is up reveal the solution slide that shows the bins. Speaking to the group how close where they? Did they get any on the mark or did they place lots of bins along the route.
  1. Move onto the next slide, with region information, ask the Young People if they though it was strange that the urban areas had lots of bins while the rural areas did not.
  1. Next move onto the next sets of maps, complete the same process for the next maps, using the knowledge that the Young People have gained from the first set of maps
  1. Finally, discuss with the Young People what they should do with their waste when they are along such a route with no bins? Would they litter or not? Evaluate what they think could be improved along these routes?

How to Build Your Own

You can design your own course with a bit of effort. Choose a local path or route that has a range of different areas (urban, rural, etc.) that is passes through. Then using Google My Maps (free) plot the route you will take.

Then walk the route, note down the location of each bin and the categorisation of each of the areas. You could use a template like this.
